The 2 Stroke

A two-stroke engine is a type of internal combustion engine that completes a power cycle with two strokes of the piston during one power cycle, this power cycle being completed in one revolution of the crankshaft. In a two-stroke engine, the end of the combustion stroke and the beginning of the compression stroke happen simultaneously, with the intake and exhaust functions occurring at the same time. Two-stroke engines often have a high power-to-weight ratio, power being available in a narrow range of rotational speeds called the power band. Two-stroke engines have fewer moving parts than four-stroke engines, and thus are cheaper to manufacture. In countries and regions with stringent emmissions regulation, two-stroke engines have been phased out in automotive and motorcycle uses. In regions where regulations are less stringent, or non-existent, small displacement two-stroke engines remain popular in mopeds and motorcycles.
